Role

Assistant journey design, information architecture, risk framing, and product requirements.

Focus

Retrieval, validation, refusal logic, escalation, trust, and stakeholder-ready AI product design.

Challenge

Useful AI assistants need trust architecture, not just prompts.

The design goal was to move beyond a chatbot that sounds confident. Nora needed to show how an assistant could identify evidence, know its boundaries, and route uncertain cases in a way that protects the user experience.

Approach

Treat trust as a product requirement.

I structured the assistant around a retrieval-first flow, validation checkpoints, refusal behavior, and human review. The system design separates what the assistant knows, what it can infer, and what must be escalated or declined.

  • Defined a source-grounded response pattern so answers can be traced to evidence.
  • Designed validation gates before final response generation.
  • Added refusal and clarification paths for unsupported or ambiguous requests.
  • Framed escalation as part of the workflow for higher-risk or low-confidence tasks.

Why it matters

The case shows how I think about AI as an operational system.

Nora helps position the portfolio for AI product and program roles. It shows that I can design assistants with user value, governance, and implementation constraints in the same conversation.
