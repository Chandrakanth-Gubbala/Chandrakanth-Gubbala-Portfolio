Role

Strategy, operating model design, financial modeling, stakeholder narrative, and commercialization planning.

Focus

Residential energy retrofits, sponsor monetization, ESG reporting, customer engagement, and scale logic.

Challenge

Retrofit programs need economics, trust, and adoption working together.

The project required more than a calculator. The model had to connect homeowners, sponsors, verification partners, and internal operating capacity into one coherent growth story.

Approach

Model the business as a platform, not a one-time service.

I structured the work around household data readiness, revenue streams, verification points, and operating constraints. The goal was to show how an energy retrofit offering could scale from a focused launch cohort to a broader operating model without losing credibility.

  • Built scale logic from roughly 200 to 900 homes to show operational and revenue implications.
  • Mapped sponsor and advertiser monetization into customer engagement and ESG reporting flows.
  • Designed verification and escrow concepts to make the model more credible for partners.
  • Translated financial assumptions into an MVP roadmap that could be discussed with nontechnical stakeholders.

Why it matters

The work connects climate impact to a business model recruiters can evaluate.

GreenPack is the clearest energy-strategy proof point in the portfolio. It shows how I think across product, finance, data, and adoption when the final recommendation has to be operationally realistic.
